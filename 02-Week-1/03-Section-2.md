Why Software Projects Are Different


Software projects are not predictable in the same way as construction or manufacturing projects.

Key Differences

- Requirements Change Frequently

    -- Users often do not know what they want at the start

    -- Feedback changes direction mid-project

- Software Is Intangible
    -- You cannot “see” progress physically

    -- 80% done often means 80% uncertainty remains

- Technology Evolves Rapidly

    -- Frameworks, tools, and best practices change constantly

- People Are the Primary Resource

    -- Productivity varies widely

    -- Communication failures kill projects faster than technical problems.
This is why traditional rigid planning fails in software.
