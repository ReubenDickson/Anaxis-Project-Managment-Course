User Personas (Why They Matter)

A user persona is a realistic representation of a typical user of the system. It is a fictional, research-based profile representing a specific user segment, detailing their goals, pain points, behaviors, and motivation. These archetypes help project teams build empathy, align on user needs and make data-driven design or development decisions.

Key Components of a User Persona:
- Name & Photo: Fictionalized to make the persona relatable.
- Demographics: Age, job title, and professional background.
- Goals & Motivations: What they want to achieve with the product.
- Pain Points & Challenges: Frustrations they face that the project should solve.
- Behaviors: How they currently work or interact with similar solutions. 

Personas help you answer:

i. Who is using the system?

ii. What do they care about?

iii. What problems are they trying to solve?

For the CBT system, you are not designing for “everyone.”
You are designing for specific roles.
