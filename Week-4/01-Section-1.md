What Are Software Requirements?

A requirement describes:

i. What a system must do
ii. How the system must behave
iii. Constraints the system must operate within

Good requirements are:

i. Clear
ii. Testable
iii. Unambiguous
iv. Aligned with user needs

Bad requirements create:

i. Rework
ii. Conflict
iii. Scope creep
iv. Missed expectations

Scope creep is the uncontrolled, unauthorized, or undocumented expansion of a projects's scope, adding features or reuirements withut adjusting time, budget, or resources. As a Project Manager, you must avoid this.
All changes to a project requirements must be properly documented in the change management process, thereby ensuring that all required resources adjustments are taken into consideration as well.
